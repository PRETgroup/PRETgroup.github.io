PRETzel
The University of Auckland

Cyber-physical systems such as biomedical devices, intelligent transportation systems, and safety-critical robots require precise timing to operate flawlessly. The focus of our research group is to explore disruptive solutions in these domains by developing precision timed architectures, C-based languages providing control over timing, and static analysis techniques for ensuring soundness of functionality and timing. We work on functional safety standards such as IEC61508 and similar standards. We have worked with many New Zealand companies working on airport baggage handling, intelligent transportation systems, and embedded systems in farming automation.

Pretzel: pret·zel· [pret-suhl]

A pretzel is a bread pastry of Medieval European origin, that has the shape of a three looped knot or twisted braid [Wikipedia].
PRETzel in our context signifies the Precision Timed (PRET) Systems research that brings together four different research groups, namely the University of Auckland in Auckland, New Zealand, the INRIA Research Lab in Grenoble, France, the Real-Time and Embedded Systems Group at Kiel University, Germany, and the Center for Hybrid and Embedded Software Systems at University of California, Berkeley, USA.
